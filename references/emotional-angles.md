# Emotional Angles for UGC Scripts

A menu of proven emotional angles for UGC short-form video scripts. Each angle has a name, the core emotion it triggers, and a brief description of how to execute it. Select 5–15 per generation based on what fits the product and audience.

---

## Identity & Self-Image

| Angle | Emotion | How to Execute |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| The Confession | Shame → Pride | Open with a personal failure that the target audience recognizes in themselves |
| The Quiet Flex | Confidence → Inspiration | Understate a real win — the anti-brag. "I just shrugged." |
| The Late Starter | Regret → Renewal | Lead with age or time lost, then reclaim it with a humble win |
| The Imposter | Fear → Legitimacy | "I felt like I didn't belong here. Then I realized no one does at first." |
| The Comparison Trap | Inadequacy → Liberation | "I kept comparing myself to [X]. Then I stopped. Here's what changed." |

## Time & Habit

| Angle | Emotion | How to Execute |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| The 5 Min Reader | Guilt → Relief | "I don't have time to study. So I stopped studying. Here's what I do instead." |
| The Speed Reader | Impatience → Progress | Use specific before/after numbers: "45 min → 8 min per page." |
| The Late Adopter | Skepticism → Conversion | "I resisted this for a year. Here's what finally convinced me." |

## Audience-Specific Angles

### Language Learners
- The JLPT Trap — passed the test, couldn't do the thing
- The Drama Fan — watched 300 hours of content, still couldn't read
- The Lonely Dictionary — spending more time in the dictionary than the book
- The 3 Systems Fear — almost quit on day one because it was too overwhelming

### Fitness / Health
- The Macro Counter — tracked everything, saw nothing
- The Gym Dropout — quit the gym 3 times, found what actually worked
- The Mirror Lie — looked worse than I felt, felt better than I looked

### Creators / Freelancers
- The First Client — almost talked myself out of sending the proposal
- The Rate Shame — charged too little for too long
- The Algorithm Despair — made great content, got 12 views

### Productivity / Work
- The Meeting Trap — productive days vs. meeting days
- The Tool Collector — downloaded 6 apps to solve one problem
- The Deadline Calm — used to panic, now I don't

## Execution Principles

1. **The hook is a confession, not a feature.** Never open with what the product does.
2. **The problem reframes, not blames.** The audience's struggle is systemic, not personal.
3. **The result is specific and humble.** "Finished one chapter" beats "became fluent."
4. **The CTA is low-friction.** One action. One hashtag cluster. Optional comment question.
5. **Director's notes are visual, not editorial.** Tell the creator what to *show*, not what to *feel*.
6. **Angles should not repeat within a batch.** Each script needs a fresh emotional entry point.
